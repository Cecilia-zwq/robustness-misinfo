   - Encourage writing down feelings and thoughts when emotions feel overwhelming.

### Communication Tools

1. **Nonviolent Communication (NVC) Framework**
   - Focuses on expressing observations, feelings, needs, and requests without blame.
   - Example: “When I see [observation], I feel [feeling] because I need [need]. Would you be willing to [request]?”
   - This approach fosters empathy and reduces defensiveness.

2. **Active Listening Practice**
   - Reflect back what you hear before responding.
   - Use phrases like “What I’m hearing is…” or “It sounds like you’re feeling…”
   - This helps the other person feel understood and can de-escalate emotional tension.

3. **“Pause and Check-In”**
   - Agree on a signal or phrase to pause conversations when emotions run high.
   - Take a short break to breathe and regroup before continuing.
