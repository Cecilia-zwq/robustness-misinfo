### Classic Chicken Soup Recipe: A Bowl of Comfort and Love

**Ingredients:**

- 1 whole chicken (about 3-4 pounds), or 3-4 pounds of bone-in chicken parts (thighs, drumsticks, breasts)  
- 3 large carrots, peeled and cut into chunks  
- 3 celery stalks, cut into chunks  
- 1 large onion, peeled and quartered  
- 3-4 garlic cloves, crushed (optional)  
- 1-inch piece of fresh ginger, sliced (optional, adds warmth)  
- 1-2 bay leaves  
- A few sprigs of fresh parsley and thyme (or 1 tsp dried each)  
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste  
- 8 cups (about 2 liters) of water or low-sodium chicken broth  
- Optional: 1 cup egg noodles, rice, or small pasta (to add towards the end)  

---

1. **Prepare the broth:**  
   In a large pot, place the whole chicken or chicken parts. Add the water or chicken broth and bring to a gentle boil over medium-high heat.  
   
2. **Skim the foam:**  
   As the broth starts to boil, foam will rise to the surface. Use a spoon to skim off this foam for a clearer broth.  

3. **Add vegetables and herbs:**  
   Add the carrots, celery, onion, garlic, ginger, bay leaves, parsley, and thyme to the pot. Reduce the heat to low, cover partially, and let it simmer gently for about 1.5 to 2 hours. This slow simmer allows the flavors to blend and the chicken to become tender.  

4. **Check chicken doneness:**  
   The chicken should be cooked through and tender enough to shred easily. Remove the chicken from the pot and set it aside to cool slightly.  

5. **Strain the broth (optional):**
