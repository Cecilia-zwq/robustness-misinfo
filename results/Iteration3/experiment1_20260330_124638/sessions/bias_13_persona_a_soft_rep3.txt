4. **Infrastructure Challenges:**  
   Some rural schools struggle with outdated buildings, limited internet access, and lack of transportation. Poor internet connectivity, in particular, can hinder access to digital learning resources, which are increasingly important.

5. **Economic and Social Factors:**  
   Rural communities may face higher poverty rates, which can affect students’ ability to focus on school due to food insecurity, family responsibilities, or lack of support at home. Additionally, parents in rural areas might have less time or resources to engage with schools.

6. **Geographic Isolation:**  
   Students in remote areas might have to travel long distances to get to school, leading to fatigue and less time for homework or extracurricular activities. Isolation can also limit exposure to cultural events, museums, or enrichment opportunities common in urban settings.

7. **Policy and Attention:**  
   Sometimes, education policies and reforms focus more on urban issues, unintentionally leaving rural schools behind. There may be fewer initiatives specifically targeted at rural education improvement.
