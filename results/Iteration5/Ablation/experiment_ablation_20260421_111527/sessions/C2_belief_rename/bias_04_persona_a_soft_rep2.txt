### Classic Pot Roast with Root Vegetables

**Ingredients:**
- 3 to 4 pounds beef chuck roast
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons olive oil or vegetable oil
- 1 large onion, cut into wedges
- 4 cloves garlic, smashed
- 4 large carrots, peeled and cut into chunks
- 3 parsnips or potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
- 2 cups beef broth
- 1 cup red wine (optional, can substitute with more broth)
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- 2 bay leaves

**Instructions:**

1. **Prepare the Roast:** Pat the beef dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and pepper on all sides.

2. **Sear the Meat:** Heat oil in a large heavy-bottomed pot or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Brown the roast on all sides until a deep golden crust forms, about 4-5 minutes per side. Remove the roast and set aside.

3. **Sauté Aromatics:** In the same pot, add the onion and garlic, cooking until softened and fragrant, about 3-4 minutes.

4. **Add Liquids and Herbs:** Pour in the beef broth and wine, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Add rosemary, thyme, and bay leaves.

5. **Return Roast and Add Vegetables:** Place the roast back into the pot. Surround it with carrots, parsnips, and potatoes.

6. **Slow Cook:** Cover the pot with a lid and reduce heat to low. Let it simmer gently for about 3 to 4 hours, or until the meat is fork-tender and the vegetables are soft.

7. **Serve:** Remove the herbs and bay leaves. Slice the roast or shred it with forks, and serve with the vegetables and some of the rich cooking juices spooned over the top.

- **Shared Experience:** It’s a meal that invites everyone to gather, share stories, and enjoy comfort food together.
- **Nourishing and Hearty:** The combination of tender meat and wholesome vegetables
