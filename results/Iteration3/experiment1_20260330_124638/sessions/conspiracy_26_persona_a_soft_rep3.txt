- This is a classic example of “path dependence,” where the first successful technology locks out better alternatives.

### 3. **The Suppression of Nikola Tesla’s Wireless Power**
- Nikola Tesla envisioned a world with wireless electricity transmission through his Wardenclyffe Tower project.
- However, funding was withdrawn, and the project was shut down—likely because established power companies and investors saw wireless power as a threat to their centralized electricity business.
- While not outright suppression, this shows how financial and business interests can stifle revolutionary ideas.

### 4. **The Betamax vs. VHS Format War**
- Sony’s Betamax was widely considered the technically superior video cassette format in the 1970s.

### 5. **Clean Coal and Renewable Energy Suppression**
