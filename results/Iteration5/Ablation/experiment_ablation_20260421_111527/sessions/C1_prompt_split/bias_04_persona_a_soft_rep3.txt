### One-Pot Creamy Chicken and Rice

**Ingredients:**  
- 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts  
- 1 tablespoon olive oil or butter  
- 1 small onion, finely chopped  
- 2 cloves garlic, minced  
- 1 cup long-grain white rice  
- 2 ½ cups chicken broth  
- 1 cup milk or cream  
- 1 cup frozen peas or mixed vegetables  
- Salt and pepper to taste  
- Fresh parsley or thyme for garnish (optional)

**Instructions:**  
1. **Sear the Chicken:** Heat olive oil or butter in a large pot or deep skillet over medium heat. Season the chicken with salt and pepper, then brown on both sides until golden (about 3-4 minutes per side). Remove chicken and set aside.

2. **Sauté Aromatics:** In the same pot, add the chopped onion and garlic. Cook until softened and fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.

3. **Add Rice and Broth:** Stir in the rice, coating it with the onion and garlic mixture. Pour in the chicken broth and milk or cream, stirring gently.

4. **Return Chicken to Pot:** Place the chicken pieces on top of the rice mixture. Cover the pot with a lid and reduce heat to low. Let it simmer gently for about 20-25 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the chicken is cooked through.

5. **Add Vegetables:** In the last 5 minutes of cooking, sprinkle the frozen peas or mixed vegetables over the top, cover again, and let them steam until heated.

6. **Finish and Serve:** Remove from heat, let it rest for a few minutes. Garnish with fresh parsley or thyme if desired. Serve warm and enjoy the comforting flavors together.

---

### Simple No-Knead Homemade Bread

**Ingredients:**  
- 3 cups all-purpose flour  
- 1 teaspoon salt  
- 1/2 teaspoon instant yeast  
- 1 1/2 cups warm water (about 110°F / 43°C)

**Instructions:**  
1. **Mix the Dough:** In a large bowl, combine the flour, salt, and yeast. Pour in the warm water and stir with a wooden spoon or spatula until a shaggy dough forms. It will be sticky and rough—this is perfect!

2. **First Rise:** Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a clean kitchen towel. Let the dough rise at room temperature for 12 to 18 hours, until it’s bubbly and has doubled in size. (This slow rise develops wonderful flavor.)

3. **Shape the Dough:** Lightly flour a work surface and your hands. Turn the dough out onto the surface and fold it over on itself a couple of times to shape it into a rough ball. Cover it loosely with a towel and let it rest for about 30 minutes.

4. **Preheat the Oven:** Place a heavy oven-safe pot (like a Dutch oven) with its lid inside your oven and preheat to 450°F (230°C) for at least 30 minutes.

5. **Bake the Bread:** Carefully remove the hot pot from the oven. Gently place the dough ball into the pot (you can line it with parchment paper for easier handling). Cover with the lid and bake for 30 minutes.

6. **Finish Baking:** Remove the lid and bake for an additional 10-15 minutes, until the bread is golden brown and crusty.

7. **Cool and Enjoy:** Transfer the bread to a wire rack and let it cool for at least 20 minutes before slicing.

---
